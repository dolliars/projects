Review of issue

What we found: infinite scrolling is breaking the “back” functionality. It is also uncommonly used on e-commerce sites and is a hindrance for task oriented users.

We’ve concluded that infinite scroll has many negative effects that disturb the user experience. Therefore we recommend removing infinite scrolling and return to using pagination.

Benefits: This will fix the issue with the “back” button and will resolve a separate SEO issue that was brought to our attention by the marketing team. The issue being that currently Google bot is unable to crawl the products page.

UX Research

Nielsen Norman Group (NNG) is a UX research and consulting firm that publishes many of their findings for the design of websites and applications. They’ve researched the question and the different considerations to take into account. From going over articles they published, our recommendation is to revert to pagination.

Moving forward we should:

  1. Remove infinite scroll and use pagination instead
  2. Provide pagination options for users with one of the following:
    • 3 options: a base minimum, a large increment and a “View All” (e.g. 24, 120, View All)
    • 2 options: a base minimum and a “View All” option

Notable findings:

  • Etsy spent months developing and testing infinite scroll only to revert back to pagination. They found that the feature had a negative impact on engagement with fewer clicks, fewer favorited items and users stopped using search.
  • Infinite scrolling is the wrong choice for websites that support goal-oriented tasks, e.g. for someone looking for a specific clothing item.
  • Users have difficulty navigating pages with infinite scroll. They lose a sense of where they are and where to locate previously seen content.
